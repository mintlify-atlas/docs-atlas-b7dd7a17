jo runs 100% locally on your Mac. Your data never leaves your device unless you explicitly choose to send a specific conversation to a cloud model.

## Core privacy guarantee

<Warning>
  **Nothing leaves your Mac.** Your notes, photos, emails, and files stay exactly where they are. Nothing is uploaded. Nothing is shared. Nothing leaves your machine. Ever.
</Warning>

### What this means

- All AI processing happens on your Mac's Neural Engine
- Your indexed data is stored in an encrypted database locally
- No external servers ever see your personal content
- No account required, no data collection, no tracking

## How we guarantee this

You don't have to take our word for it. Here's how you can verify:

### 1. No network calls for your data

Open Activity Monitor while using jo. You'll see zero outbound connections to our servers for your content.

<Tip>
  **Try it yourself:** 
  1. Open Activity Monitor (Applications → Utilities)
  2. Search for "jo" in the process list
  3. Check the Network tab
  4. Ask jo several questions about your data
  5. Verify zero outbound data transfer
</Tip>

### 2. All processing on-device

The AI model runs locally on your Mac's Neural Engine. Your queries never leave your machine.

- **Local model:** Runs entirely on your M-series chip
- **Neural Engine:** Uses dedicated AI hardware built into your Mac
- **No cloud inference:** For searches and summaries of your personal data

### 3. Verifiable architecture

jo uses Apple's native APIs (same as Spotlight). We can't access your data even if we wanted to.

<Info>
  **Open to audit:** Security researchers can verify our claims. Email security@askjo.ai for technical documentation.
</Info>

### 4. Source code transparency

We're open about how jo works:

- Documentation of all APIs used
- Network architecture diagrams available
- Privacy implementation details published
- Third-party security audits welcomed

## Optional cloud models

While jo runs locally, you can optionally use cloud models for complex tasks.

### When you might use cloud models

- Complex reasoning tasks
- Advanced analysis
- Specialized capabilities (coding, math, etc.)
- When you need extra computing power

<Note>
  **You control what's shared:** When you call a cloud model, only that specific conversation is sent—never your full indexed data.
</Note>

### Available cloud models

- GPT (OpenAI)
- Claude (Anthropic)
- Gemini (Google)
- Grok (xAI)

### Privacy with cloud models

When you use a cloud model:

1. **You explicitly choose** to send that conversation
2. **Only the conversation** is sent, not your entire data
3. **You control the context** that gets included
4. **No training on your data** — cloud providers have contracts preventing this

<Warning>
  **Local by default:** Most queries run completely locally. Cloud models are opt-in for when you need extra power.
</Warning>

## Privacy vs. other tools

### vs. ChatGPT

ChatGPT requires uploading files to external servers. jo already knows your stuff because it lives on your machine, not theirs.

**ChatGPT:**
- Lives in the cloud
- Requires uploading your files
- Your data on their servers
- Training data concerns

**jo:**
- Lives on your Mac
- Never uploads your files
- Your data stays local
- No training on your data

### vs. Rewind

Rewind records your screen (privacy nightmare). jo reads your actual files using the same APIs Apple uses.

**Rewind:**
- Records everything on your screen
- Screenshots of sensitive data
- Captures passwords, banking, etc.
- Visual recordings stored

**jo:**
- Reads actual files via Apple APIs
- No screenshots or recordings
- Respects app-level permissions
- Semantic indexing only

<Warning>
  jo doesn't take screenshots or record your screen. It reads your actual files using secure APIs, just like Spotlight does.
</Warning>

### vs. Cloud note apps

Notion, Evernote, and others store your notes on their servers.

**Cloud note apps:**
- All notes on their servers
- Subject to their privacy policies
- Potential data breaches
- Third-party access

**jo:**
- Indexes your existing Apple Notes
- Everything stays on your Mac
- You control your data
- No third-party servers

## System security

jo respects macOS's built-in security features.

### Permissions

jo asks for explicit permissions:

- **Photos:** Access to Photos library
- **Contacts:** Access to Contacts
- **Calendar:** Access to Calendar events
- **Files:** Access to specified directories

You approve each permission via macOS's standard permission dialogs.

<Info>
  **macOS protection:** If jo tries to access something without permission, macOS blocks it and prompts you to approve or deny.
</Info>

### Encryption

jo's local index is encrypted:

- **At rest:** Encrypted database on your Mac
- **In memory:** Secure memory handling
- **No plain text:** Sensitive data never stored in plain text

### App sandboxing

jo runs in macOS's app sandbox:

- Limited system access
- Can't access other users' data
- Restricted to approved directories
- Monitored by macOS security

## Uninstalling jo

When you uninstall jo:

1. **Index deleted:** All indexed data is removed
2. **Original files safe:** Your Notes, Photos, Mail, etc. are untouched
3. **No traces:** No data left on external servers (there are none)
4. **Complete removal:** Standard Mac app uninstall

<Info>
  **Clean uninstall:** Simply drag jo to the Trash. All indexed data goes with it. Your original data in Apple Notes, Photos, Mail, etc. remains exactly as it was.
</Info>
