jo is ready to help the moment you finish installation. Unlike traditional search tools, jo understands meaning and context—not just exact keywords.

## How to ask jo a question

<Steps>
  <Step title="Launch jo">
    Press **Cmd+Space** (or your configured shortcut) to open jo from anywhere on your Mac. The query interface appears instantly, ready for your question.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Ask naturally">
    Type your question just as you'd ask a person. No special syntax or keywords needed. jo understands natural language.
    
    <Tip>Start with simple questions about recent activity to see how jo works.</Tip>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review the answer">
    jo searches your notes, emails, photos, calendar, and files to provide a complete answer. Each answer includes source references so you can verify the information.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Example queries to try

Here are real queries that jo handles well. These examples show how to ask about different aspects of your digital life:

### Email and communication

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Catch me up on my emails with the accountant">
    jo scans your inbox for recent threads with your accountant, summarizes the conversation topics, and highlights any action items or pending responses.
    
    **What jo accesses:** Mail app via IMAP
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Did my landlord ever get back to me about the leak?">
    jo searches your email history for messages from your landlord about the leak, shows you the latest response, and tells you if you're waiting on them or they're waiting on you.
    
    **What jo accesses:** Mail app conversations
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="What did I tell Jake I'd send him?">
    jo reviews your recent messages and emails with Jake to find any commitments or promises you made.
    
    **What jo accesses:** Mail, Messages
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Files and documents

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Where's that W-9 from my accountant?">
    jo searches your Downloads, Documents, and email attachments for W-9 files from your accountant.
    
    **What jo accesses:** Local files, email attachments
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Help me pull together my competitor notes">
    jo finds all your notes, documents, and browser bookmarks related to competitors and presents them in one place.
    
    **What jo accesses:** Notes, files, browser history
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Browsing and research

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What was that productivity podcast I started?">
    jo checks your browser history, saved bookmarks, and notes for productivity podcasts you've visited or mentioned.
    
    **What jo accesses:** Browser history, Notes
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Where's that Stripe page I had open?">
    jo searches your recent browser history for Stripe documentation or dashboard pages.
    
    **What jo accesses:** Browser history
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Meetings and calendar

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What came out of my 1:1 with Sarah?">
    jo finds meeting notes, calendar entries, and follow-up emails related to your meeting with Sarah.
    
    **What jo accesses:** Calendar, Notes, Mail
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Prep me for my 2pm with Sarah Chen">
    jo checks your calendar, finds your last 5 email threads with Sarah, pulls up notes from previous meetings, and surfaces relevant context like personal details she's mentioned.
    
    **What jo accesses:** Calendar, Mail, Notes
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## What makes a good query

jo works best when you ask questions the way you naturally think about information:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Be specific about people" icon="user">
    Include names, roles, or relationships. "My landlord" or "Jake" helps jo narrow the search.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Mention timeframes" icon="clock">
    Add context like "last week," "this month," or "from 2022" when it matters.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Describe the topic" icon="tag">
    Use your own words. jo understands "budget issue" even if your email said "spending problem."
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Ask follow-ups" icon="comments">
    jo remembers context within a conversation. Ask "What about the other contractors?" after an initial query.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Local vs. cloud queries

Most queries run entirely on your Mac using the local AI model. jo only uses cloud models when:

- You explicitly request advanced help ("Call in GPT for this")
- The query requires complex reasoning or synthesis
- You're working on a creative task like drafting or brainstorming

<Note>
**Privacy first:** Local queries never leave your Mac. When you do use a cloud model, only that specific conversation is sent—never your indexed data.
</Note>

## Tips for better results

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Start broad, then narrow" icon="filter">
    Begin with a general question, then ask follow-ups to drill down. "Show me my Tokyo trip photos" followed by "Just the restaurant photos."
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Use context clues" icon="lightbulb">
    Include details you remember: "That email with the contractor quote, I think it was around $4,000."
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Ask about connections" icon="link">
    jo excels at finding relationships: "What projects am I working on with both Sarah and Mike?"
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Request summaries" icon="list">
    Ask jo to synthesize information: "Summarize what I learned at that conference last year."
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
